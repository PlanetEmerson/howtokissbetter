The Night Everything Changed

I was fifteen. She was older. She had a car, and confidence I couldn't comprehend.

We drove to a park. Talked about nothing. Came back to her car. The windows started to fog. My heart was pounding so hard I was sure she could hear it.

Then it happened. Our lips met.

For three seconds, I thought I might be okay. Then I realized I was drowning.

She was good. Not just good. Confident. Her kisses had variety, rhythm, intention. There were sub-types of kisses I didn't know existed. And I was lost in a language I had never learned.

"I could feel her enthusiasm dimming in real-time, like a light on a dimmer switch being slowly turned down."

The drive home was silent. She didn't turn off the engine when we arrived. The door closed behind me with a sound I can still hear. Not a slam, just a definitive thunk. A period at the end of a sentence.

She never spoke to me again.

I stood on my driveway in the dark. It had started to rain. Of course it had started to rain.

"Here's what nobody tells you: a kiss delivers its verdict in seconds. And once that verdict is delivered, it's almost impossible to appeal."

That night, I made a decision: I would never be caught unprepared again.

What followed were years of obsessive research, experimentation, and eventually, a career I never expected.
